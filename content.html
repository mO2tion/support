Training
What is watt (power)?

Watt measures your energy output and is a measure of how hard you work. Watt measures your performance instantly whereas heart rate only shows how hard it feels for your body, and it has a delay of up to several minutes. Heart rate can be affected by other external factors such as room temperature, stress, time of day and other variables. Therefore, watt is a more precise metric. You can follow your progress by seeing how the watt you can output at a given heart rate change over time.

What is FTP?

FTP is your Functional Threshold Power. This is the maximum watt you can deliver for one hour. If you do not know your FTP, you can consult this approximate table. You can also take a 5-minute test at full power seated and multiply the average power by 0.82 to get your FTP.

What is watt/weight?

This is a number cyclists are particularly interested in. Your speed uphill is mainly dependent on how many watts you can put into your pedals per kg of body weight. Professionals can go up to 6 watts/kg over 20 minutes while untrained riders usually deliver around 2 watts/kg.

Setup
As an instructor, how can I use broadcast my data live to the rest of the team?

You can broadcast your data as a special line on the recording screen to make it easier for the rest of the team to follow your lead. Just let us know on support@motosumo.com that you are an instructor and we will make sure to set it up for you. When that is done, you will get access to a special instructor menu in the app (the three dots in the top right corner of the recording screen) that allows you to toggle your data on and off for the rest of the team.

Ok, I registered as an instructor, how can I use the app to make my class even more engaging?

After you have registered as an instructor, we make a number of tools available for you in the instructor menu in the app:

  • Broadcast your data live: Just click start broadcasting, and your line (heart rate, cadence, or watt) will show up on the team members phone. On some screens of the group display, you will also get a nifty badge to make you stand out!
  • Start a timer: if you click start timer in the instructor menu, you can send out either short countdown timers og long segment timers. These timers show up on your team members screens, and on the group display (if you are using that -- and we really think you should!).
  • Send out some emojis: Select up to three emojis, and they will show up on your team members phones and on the group display. It's a fun way to motivate or reward your team, or just lighten the mood in the room.

Why does Motosumo need access to the internet to work?

To translate the signals from your phone into a cadence value, they need to be processed by our algorithm in the cloud. So, to allow the signals to be sent to the cloud and the cadence back to the phone, Motosumo needs access to an internet connection.

How should I position my phone on the bike?

The phone needs to pick up the vibrations from the bike in order to calculate your cadence. For that to work, the phone needs to be placed on the handlebars of the bike in a stable and forward pointing position. If the phone is too wobbly, the signals will get corrupted and you will get a wrong cadence measurement.

Why does the fixture on the bike matter?

Motosumo uses the vibrations of the bike to calculate your cadence. In order to pick up those signals, the fixture for your phone has to be of a certain quality in order to give a seamless experience. Most importantly, the phone has to be put in a stable position (i.e., not wobbly) and point forward.

How can I avoid incoming calls when training?

If you wish to avoid incoming calls while you are training, you can activate "Do not disturb" on your phone. Here's how to do it:
iPhone: Swipe up from the bottom of the screen to open up Control Center. There, click on the "Do not disturb" icon (it looks like a crescent moon). Remember to switch it off again when you're done training!
Android: Swipe down from the top of the screen to open up "Quick Settings". In the top bar of icons, activate "Do not disturb" (the icon is a circle with a horizontal line inside it). To access the "Do not disturb" option in the Quick Settings menu, you might have to expand the menu and/or swipe to see additional settings.

What is the difference between Do not disturb and Flight mode?

Do not disturb will block incoming calls so you can train without being disturbed. Flight mode will block all data access which means Motosumo will stop working. If you wish to avoid disturbances while using Motosumo, always use Do not disturb , not Flight mode .

How does Motosumo measure my cadence?

Motosumo uses the sensors of your smartphone to measure your cadence while biking. While you bike, the smartphone sensors (the same ones that make sure to rotate your screen) can pick up the vibrations of your bike. These signals are then sent to our calculation server where we run them through our cadence algorithm. The result is then returned to your phone and showed on the display. To get proper signals for our cadence algorithm, the phone needs to be securely fastened to the handlebars of the bike in a forward pointing and flat position.

Hardware
How can I connect to a heart rate monitor?

Make sure Bluetooth is activated on your phone.
Then press on the record button in the app and then on the button with a heart on it (next to the GO button). Put on your heart rate monitor and select it from the list in the app. You have to wear it to connect. You have successfully connected to your heart rate monitor when you see a checkmark next to it on the list. If you can‚Äôt see your heart rate monitor on the list after about 10 seconds, then make sure it is switched on and/or try to moisten its connection pads and make sure that the battery is charged.

Which heart rate monitors are compatible with Motosumo?

You can use any heart rate monitor that transmits via Bluetooth. Most modern heart rate monitors will do this. If you are in doubt, you can always write to us at support@motosumo.com.

Why can't I see my heart rate monitor in the app?

There are usually three reasons why this can fail:

  1. Check that your heart rate monitor transmits via Bluetooth. If it doesn't, then you can't connect it in the app.
  2. Make sure that your heart rate monitor is switched on. If you have a chest strap, try to moisten the connection pads before wearing it as this will help to activate the strap. If you have a heart rate monitor with a switch, make sure the switch is on.
  3. Check that the battery is charged. You can always try to change the battery to see if it helps.

If none of the above works, send an email to support@motosumo.com and we will do our best to help you out.

How can I connect to a bike power meter?

Make sure Bluetooth is activated on your phone. Then press on the record button in the app and then on the button with a lightning on it (next to the GO button). Switch on your power monitor. If you are using a power bike, this is usually done by pedalling. When your power monitor gets shown in the app, select it on the list to pair with it. You have successfully connected to your power monitor when you see a checkmark next to it on the list. Typically the name of the bike is shown on a sticker on the bike, often it has a number such as #12.

Which bike power meters are compatible with Motosumo?

You can use any bike power meter that transmits via Bluetooth. Most modern power meters will do this. If you are in doubt, you can always write to us at support@motosumo.com. We have tested the following Bluetooth devices to be compatible with Motosumo:

  1. PowerTap pedals
  2. Body Bike Smart+
  3. Wattbike
  4. Schwinn bikes
We also support most Stages bikes and Tomahawk bikes. Keiser and Spinning (Precor) bikes are in the pipeline.

Group Display
What is the group display?

The group display offers an opportunity to get a full blown group training experience by displaying training data on a large display. As an instructor, you can use it to coach the team. As a regular member, you can use it to get an overview of how your teammates are doing. The group display can show either the cadence, heart rate or power (if you have a bluetooth heart rate or power monitor connected in the app) of the individual members of the team, or a dedicated team mode showing how well the team is performing.

What does the group display show?

The group display can show either the cadence, heart rate, or cycling power (if you have a Bluetooth heart rate or power monitor connected in the app) of your team. This is perfect if you want to have an overview of the entire team and how they are performing. There is also a team mode that focuses on the team rather than the individuals. Here, you can, for example, see how well the team is synced (based on cadence measurements).

How do I access the group display?

Go to https://team.motosumo.com using the Google Chrome browser and log in using the same credentials (email and password) as in the app. The browser will then wait for you to start a new session in the app. When you do so, the browser will display the group display.

What do I need to try out the group display?

You will need the following to use the group display.

  1. A computer or tablet connected to the internet.
  2. The Google Chrome web browser ( https://www.google.com/chrome/). This is the only browser we guarantee support for.
  3. Optionally a large external monitor or projector connected to the computer if you want to display the group display to your team.

Connect the external display to the computer or tablet. Open up Google Chrome and go to https://team.motosumo.com. Log in using the same credentials (email and password) as in the app. The browser will then wait for you to start a new session in the app. When you do so, the browser will display the group display.

How can I reset the group display?

Open the menu by pressing on the three horizontal lines in the upper left corner of the group display. In the menu, press Reset. This will reset the timer and the calorie count. If you are registered as an instructor, you can also reset the group display from your phone -- just select "Reset team display" in the instructor menu.

Which browsers support the group display?

Currently, the group display is only supported in the Google Chrome browser. You can download it for free here: https://www.google.com/chrome/. By supporting only one browser, we can spend more time developing new features for the group display.

Troubleshooting
I get bad cadence measurements, what should I do?

Make sure the phone is mounted correctly. The phone needs to pick up the vibrations from the bike in order to calculate your cadence. For that to work, the phone needs to be placed on the handlebars of the bike in a stable and forward pointing position. If the phone is too wobbly, the signals will get corrupted and you will get a wrong cadence measurement. Use an official phone mount for your bike, or contact us for help finding a 3. party mount that will work. The quality of your cadence data also depends on the sensors in your phone. All iPhones work fine, but some lower end android phones without gyro give more noise results. Search for the phone model on www.phonearena.com and look for Gyroscope (typically next to Accelerometer, under phone features). In general, the app still works without a gyro, but phone mounting becomes even more important. If you have a very bad internet connection, you might also get holes in your data, and the cadence data you do get will be of low quality: contact Motosumo to debug if the internet connection could be an issue.

I can't see my team!

Make sure location is enabled. Make sure you have selected the correct gym and activity (heart rate or bike) on the "start recording" screen. If you still can't see your team, select your gym manually by clicking the selected location (even if it looks right on the "start recording" screen). It can be done by selecting another gym and the picking the right gym immediately after.

I can't access the instructor menu

The menu is visible as three dots in top right corner of the "recording screen". If you don't see it, contact support@motosumo.com to make sure you are registered as an instructor. You need to include the email address you use for signing in to the app in your mail. If you know the account you have signed up with is registered as an instructor, try force closing the app (iOS, Android), and starting a new session.

My training history is not correct.

Contact support@motosumo.com, include the following information: the email address you use to sign up in the app, the time and location of the session that is either missing or flawed. A description of what is wrong with the session.

I am experiencing delays, e.g. a spiky graph or timers showing up late etc.

This is probably due to the quality of the internet connection. Try connecting to the gym wifi, or try using mobile data. You can ask your gym manager for a dedicated wifi for group training. Here at Motosumo we might not be able to do much about it, but we would be very happy if you would drop a mail to support@motosumo.com. Please include the email address you use for signing up in the app, the time and geographical location (gym name) of the problematic session and a description of the problem you experienced.

My app is crashing, can you help!

Yes, we will try to fix the bug, but there are some things you can do to help us, and some things you can try to do to resolve the issue yourself. First, send an email to support@motosumo.com to inform us about the problem, even if we are already aware of the problem it is helpful for us to get more information about who is having the problem, and what triggered it. Include the following information in the email: Describe the problem. What happened immediately before the problem occurred the first time (did you do something unusual, did the app update, anything you think could have triggered the crash), your email address you used for signing up in the app, the phone make and model, when you experienced the problem the first time, A screenshot of the problem (if possible). Try to delete the app data: instructions for android 6.0, iOS, and restarting the app. This will require you to sign in to the app again. Delete the app and reinstall it from the play or app store. Hang tight, if you have emailed us a description of the crash, we will let you know if we have found and fixed the bug in the app.

I can't connect to my Bluetooth heart rate belt, what can I do?

Make sure Bluetooth is turned on, on your phone. Try closing the app and turning Bluetooth off and on again making sure your phone "sees" the device when scanning. Make sure you haven't paired with the device from another device (or someone else has), another app on your phone, or in the system Bluetooth settings on your phone os. Check that the device is Bluetooth 4.0 or Bluetooth LE compatible. Some devices, such as the Fitbit, apple watch, etc. are not standard bluetooth devices, and we don't support them yet. Feel free to drop a mail to support@motosumo.com to inform us that you are having issues with your device. Describe the exact make and model of your phone and Bluetooth device and a description of how you are experiencing the problem (what did you try to do?).
